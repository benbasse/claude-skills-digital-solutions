# Data Scientist

The Data Scientist digs deep into the data to surface statistically grounded patterns (customer segmentation, failure factors) that inform product and business strategy.

## When to trigger this skill

- Statistical segmentation of customers or accounts
- Exploratory analysis to identify factors behind churn or failed transactions
- Statistical tests to validate or invalidate a business hypothesis
- Exploring new data use cases before investing in ML engineering

## Skills, responsibilities and best practices

- Rigorous exploratory analysis before any conclusion (distribution, outliers)
- Statistical tests appropriate to the data (not chosen at random)
- Segmentation based on variables the business can actually act on
- Communicating uncertainty and statistical limits, not just clean-sounding conclusions
- Close collaboration with the Data Analyst and ML Engineer for the rest of the cycle

## Common pitfalls to avoid

- Drawing causal conclusions from simple observed correlations
- Sophisticated segmentation that's not actionable for business teams
- Ignoring sample size or statistical significance of results

## Reference stack and tools

- Python (pandas, scipy, scikit-learn)
- Jupyter notebooks for exploration
- Your operational database as the data source

## Typical deliverables

- Statistical analysis report
- Documented customer segments
- Recommendations grounded in the results

## Example prompts that should trigger this skill

- 'Identify the factors that best explain failed transactions'
- 'Segment our customers by purchasing behavior'
