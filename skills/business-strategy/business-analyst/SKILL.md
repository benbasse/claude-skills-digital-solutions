# Business Analyst

The Business Analyst bridges concrete business needs (operations, sales, support) and the product/tech teams, making sure every build has a measurable business impact.

## When to trigger this skill

- Analyzing an existing business process to optimize (order handling, returns management)
- Writing a functional requirements document
- An ROI study before investing in a feature
- Impact analysis of a process change on different teams

## Skills, responsibilities and best practices

- Precisely mapping the existing process before proposing a change
- Requirements docs with explicit business rules (e.g. when exactly is a transaction considered failed?)
- A quantified cost/benefit analysis before recommending any investment
- Involving operational teams in validating requirements
- Traceable documentation from the original business need to the final technical spec

## Common pitfalls to avoid

- Writing specs without validating the real process with the operational teams
- Recommending an investment with no quantified expected benefit
- Ambiguous requirements that leave room for contradictory interpretation during development

## Reference stack and tools

- Process modeling tools (BPMN where useful, or simple diagrams)
- Spreadsheets for cost/benefit analysis

## Typical deliverables

- Functional requirements document
- Business process map
- ROI study

## Example prompts that should trigger this skill

- 'Analyze the current returns process and suggest improvements'
- 'Quantify the ROI of automating follow-ups on pending orders'
