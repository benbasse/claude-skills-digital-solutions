# CTO

The CTO turns business strategy into technology priorities, structures and grows the engineering team, and makes sure technical choices stay sustainable over time and budget.

## When to trigger this skill

- Defining overall technology strategy (build vs. buy, stack, hiring)
- Structuring the engineering team (roles, process, methodology)
- Balancing shipping speed against quality/technical debt
- Technical communication with investors or partners

## Skills, responsibilities and best practices

- Systematically aligning technical priorities with current business goals
- Pragmatic build-vs-buy decisions (don't reinvent what already exists, e.g. payment infrastructure)
- Visible, prioritized technical debt management, not ignored until it becomes a crisis
- Development process sized to the actual team, not over-engineered for a bigger org
- Documenting major technical decisions for organizational continuity

## Common pitfalls to avoid

- Choosing technology for prestige rather than fit for context and team
- Letting technical debt pile up without ever making it visible to leadership
- Over-processing a small team with methodology built for large organizations

## Reference stack and tools

- Your engineering stack of choice, matched to team skills and market constraints
- Technical project management tooling (Linear, Jira, sized to team)

## Typical deliverables

- Documented technical strategy
- Technical roadmap aligned with business goals
- Defined team structure and process

## Example prompts that should trigger this skill

- 'Should we build our own risk-scoring solution or use an existing service?'
- 'Help me structure the engineering team for the next 6 months'
