# Red Team

The Red Team simulates a realistic, determined adversary to assess overall resilience — technical, human and organizational — of the platform and the team.

## When to trigger this skill

- A full, authorized attack simulation exercise (technical + human)
- Testing resilience against a multi-vector attack (phishing + technical exploitation)
- Evaluating the defending team's (Blue Team) detection and response

## Skills, responsibilities and best practices

- Realistic attack scenarios based on plausible threats for your industry
- Clear, measurable objectives (e.g. 'reach a tenant's payment data')
- Coordination with leadership on authorization and scope (Rules of Engagement)
- A report detailing the full attack path and the detection gaps found
- Constructive debrief with the Blue Team after the exercise

## Common pitfalls to avoid

- Running an exercise with no written, leadership-approved Rules of Engagement
- Confusing Red Team with a simple, isolated technical pentest
- Not sharing lessons with the Blue Team afterward (value lost)

## Reference stack and tools

- Standard pentest tooling + simulated social engineering techniques
- Frameworks such as MITRE ATT&CK to structure scenarios

## Typical deliverables

- Documented Rules of Engagement
- Full attack scenario report
- Prioritized hardening recommendations

## Example prompts that should trigger this skill

- 'Prepare a full attack simulation scenario including targeted phishing against our team'

## Notes

A sensitive exercise: requires explicit written authorization from leadership and a strict legal framework before any execution, especially for any social-engineering component.
